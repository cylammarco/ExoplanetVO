Sandy Rogers

My own astronomy interests are in the early Universe, when the first galaxies began to form. During my PhD at the ROE, I studied the types of stars that formed in these early galaxies, mainly using data from the Hubble Space Telescope. I also spent time as a member of the team who tested the Mid-Infrared-Instrument for Hubbleâ€™s successor, the James Webb Space Telescope.

While the galaxies I was looking at are billions of years separated from any of the exoplanets we can observe, both of those telescopes are also used in exo-planetary research!

Following my PhD, I became the Chief Scientist at London-based people analytics startup Saberr.com, where I combine data science with some software development.

Building the EXO app was my introduction to a lot of "best practice" software development - source control, and the like - that I now use every day to collaborate with the rest of our company on our commercial apps and fun hacks :)
