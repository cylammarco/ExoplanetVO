About us

ROECoders - the IfA Young Academic Software Development Team

The ROECoders were founded in 2012 by Emma Curtis-Lake and Duncan Forgan using funding from the Roberts Fund for Researcher-Led Initiatives.

The group is tasked with writing software for use in astronomy outreach, doing so in a way that mimics how software companies conceive and develop apps for commerical distribution. Currently, the group is working on developing a smartphone app for Android (EVO) that describes and explains the science of extrasolar planet detection.
