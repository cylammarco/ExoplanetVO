Create Planetary Systems

It might be harder than you think...

It might be harder than you think to set up a system that is stable over long times with multiple planets. The simulator used in this app takes account of the gravity exerted by each body in the system. So, what happens to the orbits of the other planets when you remove Jupiter from the Solar System (one of the pre-made systems available to investigate)? What happens to smaller planets when a big planet is placed on a similar orbit? The app was set up so that you can learn the answers to these questions by playing with your own systems.

Observe Planetary Systems

How to astronomers search for exoplanets? There has been a great step forwards in our understanding of how many other worlds there are in our Galaxy after the successful Kepler mission, but what types of planets get detected and how?

In this app we show two methods that astronomers use to search for exoplanets; transits and radial velocities. The signals detected update in real time with the system displayed above, so you can tip and tilt the system and see how things change.

RV

We can actually search for signatures showing the star being flung around by the gravitational influence of the planets orbiting it.

Transits

We can also search for a dip in the star's light as it is partially obscured by the planet passing in front of it.
