EVO for Science Educators

EVO Educators Guide to come soon, complete with user guide and suggested activities. The following is a brief description of possible application of the app for use by Science Educators:

The app contains on-screen instructions and explanations which allow independent learning about planetary systems and exoplanet detection methods. Educators working with groups of learners could use the app as a jumping-off point for study of related topics, within structured learning activities, or as a resource for extension work.

The content of the EVO app links to curriculum areas in both physics/science and maths for students aged 12-18 throughout the UK. As the app provides realistic data in graphical form, itâ€™s best suited for use with students who are studying or have mastered the interpretation of line graphs.

For younger students, it might be particularly useful in cross-curricular work on science and maths. Discovery of alien worlds is an exciting jumping off point for studying graphs and the physics behind the transit method is easily accessible to younger students, especially if a physical model is used to illustrate the technique.

For older students, the focus on how detections are made may usefully enhance study of how empirical research works and how knowledge of our Universe has developed in recent years. Material in the app will also be specifically applicable to topics in advanced-level physics including orbits/gravitation and the Doppler shift.

Finally, educators working with informal audiences may find it a useful interactive tool to use with passing audiences in science busking activities, open days and science fairs or as an enhancement or follow-up element to a public lecture or event featuring exoplanets.

Existing exoplanet resources for schools

ROE Exoplanets activity - available online

Deep Space Activities - Exoplanets

Students make physical models of real exoplanet systems using card and plasticine, note the location of the 'Goldilocks zone', and comment on habitability. Covers what exoplanets are, graphing skills and what makes planets habitable.

ROG Exploring Exoplanets activity - onsite session with online resources available

Exploring Exoplanets (KS3)

Exploring Exoplanets (KS4)

Exploring Exoplanets (Post-16)

Depending on level, students make physical models of exoplanets using light, ball and light sensor to create their own transit graphs or discuss RV or direct imaging methods. They go on to do calculations based on real data to determine properties of exoplanets and comment on habitability. Online resources cover calculations to, e.g., determine radius and volume, of planets. Covers what exoplanets are, detection methods, graphing skills, what makes planets habitable, deriving additional properties from detection observations.

Planet Hunters resources on ZooTeach

(use toggle at side to filter on Planet Hunters, then click 'resources' to get list of resources)

A collection of various resources which relate to using Planet Hunters in the classroom - of varying quality and relevance. Most have been submitted by teachers in the US, but some cover subject areas relevant to UK students.

Las Cumbres Observatory Global Telescope Network project

Agent Exoplanet

Extensive online site guiding (registered) users to make brightness measurements of stars with known exoplanets as well as calibration stars to build up a transit plot. Goes on to derive exoplanet properties from the plot, with good, clear explanations of how this is done. Not specifically for schools, but very well suited for them.
